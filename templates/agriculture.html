Sustainable Agriculture & Food Security

Sustainable agriculture meets the needs of present and future generations, ensuring profitability, environmental health, and social equity. It underpins all four pillars of food security—availability, access, utilization, stability—and drives progress toward global development goals. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

What Is Sustainable Agriculture?

According to FAO, sustainable agricultural development is “the management and conservation of the natural resource base, and the orientation of technological and institutional change in such a manner as to ensure…human needs for present and future generations. Such development…conserves land, water, plant and animal genetic resources, is environmentally non‐degrading, technically appropriate, economically viable and socially acceptable.” :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

FAO’s Sustainable Food and Agriculture (SFA)

FAO’s SFA framework promotes integrated approaches across crops, livestock, forestry and fisheries to ensure responsible management of natural resources, while delivering economic and social benefits. It emphasizes five key principles: increasing productivity and value addition; protecting resources; improving livelihoods; enhancing resilience; and adaptive governance. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Millennium Development Goals & Agriculture

FAO’s most direct contribution to the MDGs was Goal 1: eradication of extreme poverty and hunger, supported by its technical expertise in agriculture, forestry, fisheries and resource management. FAO also guided efforts toward MDG 7 (environmental sustainability) by promoting sustainable practices that conserve ecosystems while enhancing food production. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Sustainable Development Goals & Agriculture

In the 2030 Agenda, food and agriculture lie at the heart of the SDGs. SDG 2 (Zero Hunger) explicitly targets sustainable production systems (Target 2.4), and Indicator 2.4.1 measures the proportion of agricultural area under productive and sustainable practices. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

  • SDG 2.4: By 2030, ensure sustainable food production systems and resilient practices that maintain ecosystems and improve land and soil quality (Indicator 2.4.1). :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
  • SDG 12.2: Sustainably manage natural resources and halve per capita global food waste. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Key Practices for Sustainability

Agroecology

Agroecology applies ecological principles to farming, optimizing local resources and biodiversity, reducing chemical inputs, and strengthening ecosystem services like pollination and soil health. It supports livelihoods, gender equity, and resilience to climate change. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Climate‑Smart Agriculture

Climate‑Smart Agriculture aims to sustainably increase productivity, build resilience and reduce greenhouse gas emissions through integrated land‑use approaches, drought‑tolerant varieties, improved water management and carbon‑sequestration practices. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Sustainable Intensification

Sustainable Crop Production Intensification (SCPI) helps countries boost yields on existing farmland via conservation agriculture, integrated plant nutrient management and ecosystem approaches—supporting food security without expanding land use. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

Data & Statistics

FAOSTAT, FAO’s corporate statistical database, reports that global crop yields have grown by over 150 % since 1961, while the share of agricultural land under sustainable management (Indicator 2.4.1) is now monitored in over 90 countries. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}
